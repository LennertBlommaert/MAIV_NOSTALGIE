Nico

It has been almost 30 years since Christa Päffgen - better known as Nico - died at the age of 49. She is known for her time as a vocalist in the Velvet Underground’s debut, but she was also a composer, fashion model, actress and above all, a big influence on several of the greatest artistic talents of her time.

Nico's story

And her influence as a muse

  • Coco chanel
  • Siouxsie and the Banshees

Nico was born the October 16th 1938 in Cologne, Germany. During the war, in which she lost her father due to sustained head injuries that caused severe brain damage, Nico moved to Spreewald forest outside of Berlin together with her family.

She was discovered at 16 by the photographer Herbert Tobias while both were working at a KaDeWe fashion show in Berlin. He gave her the name Nico after her ex-boyfriend, filmmaker Nikos Papatakis, and she used it for the rest of her life.

She moved to Paris and began working for a vast amount of fashion magazines such as Vogue, Tempo, Vie Nuove, Mascotte Spettacolo, Camera, Elle, and others..

Nico &

Coco Chanel

Nico has been beautiful since she was a child. After moving to Paris she also got noticed soon by the famous Coco Chanel, Coco Chanel took personal interest in her androgynous protégee as she was very inspired by her beauty.

To further her career and to escape Chanel’s attentions, Nico fled to New York City and abandoned the job.

Albums

III. Desertshore

IV. The End...

In the 70’s Nico alternated more and more between her film career and her music career. She played in multiple movies and also released two more solo albums Desertshore (1970) and The End… (1974).

She wrote the music, sang, and played the harmonium. John Cale produced and played most of the other instruments on both albums.

These albums, along with The Marble Index, are now considered a trilogy.

Nico &

SIOUXSIE AND THE BANSHEES

Nico briefly toured as supporting act for the Proto-gothic rock band Siouxsie and the Banshees. Siouxsie and the Banshees were one of many post-punk bands who admired Nico.

Nico’s album the marble index is sometimes described as "the first Goth album". With its stark sound, somber lyrics, and Nico’s deliberate change in her look, the album became a crucial music and visual prototype for the gothic rock movement. Gothic rock also creates a dark atmosphere by drawing influence from the drones used by the Velvet Underground. which is sometimes described as a protopunk band.

Siouxsie Sioux’ foreboding vocals are influenced by Nico’s distinct dark style of singing.
