The Muse

Nico

Nico has inspired and influenced many legendary artist over the years. From photographers and fashion designers, to film makers, conceptual artists and musicians.

She was a composer, fashion model, actress but above all, she had a great influence on the greatest of her generation.

    Nico's story

    And her influence as a muse

    Germany and Paris

    Nico was born the October 16th 1938 in Cologne, Germany. During the war, in which she lost her father due to sustained head injuries that caused severe brain damage, Nico moved to Spreewald forest outside of Berlin together with her family.

    She was discovered at 16 by the photographer Herbert Tobias while both were working at a KaDeWe fashion show in Berlin. He gave her the name Nico after her ex-boyfriend, filmmaker Nikos Papatakis, and she used it for the rest of her life.

    She moved to Paris and began working for a vast amount of fashion magazines such as Vogue, Tempo, Vie Nuove, Mascotte Spettacolo, Camera, Elle, and others..

    Nico &

    Coco Chanel

    Nico has been beautiful since she was a child. After moving to Paris she also got noticed soon by the famous Coco Chanel, Coco Chanel took personal interest in her androgynous protégee as she was very inspired by her beauty.

    To further her career and to escape Chanel’s attentions, Nico fled to New York City and abandoned the job.

    New York

    Once in New York and abondoned the job she began working for Eileen Ford.

    In the meantime she appeared in several television advertisements and she got some small roles in film productions.

    While living in New York she also took acting classes with Lee Strasberg, sharing the same class as Marilyn Monroe.

    Nico &

    Frederico Felini

    In 1959, Nico was invited to the set of Federico Fellini’s La Dolce Vita, where she attracted the attention of the acclaimed director. Mesmerized by her beauty, Fellini immediatly wrote an extra role out for her. She would play as a friend of Marcello - the main character - as herself as Fellini found that her own character was most interesting as it was.

    Ari

    In 1962 - at the age of 24 - Nico gave birth to her son, Christian Aaron “Ari” Päffgen, commonly held to have been fathered by French actor Alain Delon, although Delon always denied his paternity.

    At about the same time Nico got into music through her friendship with Rolling Stone Brian Jones and Stones manager SNrew Loog Oldham. Together with Brian Jones and Jimmy Page, she recorded her first single. I'm not sayin' for Andrew Loog Oldman's label.

    Morissey likes playing this song before the beginning of his gigs.

    Nico &

    Bob Dylan

    Dylan was introduced to Nico in Paris. After treating Dylan to a meal at her flat, Nico accompanied Dylan across Europe, a trip that passed through Germany before ending in Vernilya, a small village outside of Athens, Greece.

    Dylan stayed at Vernilya for more than a week, finishing many of the songs that would appear on his fourth and upcoming album. Dylan also completed another song called “I’ll Keep It With Mine”, which was about Nico and Ari (who he used to babysit). He gave the song to Nico, who eventually recorded it for her album, Chelsea Girl, released in 1967.

    New York

    While traveling to London Nico got in contact with Andy Warhol’s Entourage, When she went back to New York in 1966 Andy Warhol and Nico got in contact with each other.

    Nico &

    Andy Warhol

    When Nico first met Andy Warhol in a Mexican restaurant, Warhol was captivated by her and so was his companion Paul Morrissey. They were convinced to cast her in in their experimental movie projects.

    Warhol took her in at the Factory and Nico became a Warhol Superstar. These Superstars were unique personalities that appeared in Warhol’s artworks and accompanied him in his social life. In addition to the film of her singing with the Velvet Underground at the Factory, she also went on to appear in The Chelsea Girls and Imitation of Christ which was originally part of **** (Four Stars) or the twenty-five hour movie. Warhol also used her voice as the soundtrack for Sunset - which he shot in 1967.

    The Factory

    Andy Warhol and Paul Morissy were convinced that they had to find a rock group to play for her.

    They made her the vocalist of The Velvet underground. She was described by them as ‘the most beautiful creature that ever lived’.

    Nico &

    The Velvet Underground

    Nico was introduced to the avant-garde rock band The Velvet Underground by Andy Warhol, who was its manager at the time. At Warhol’s insistence, Nico sang with the band on three songs of their debut album, The Velvet Underground & Nico. The songs were specially tuned for Nico’s deadpen voices wich propelled the songs.

    At the time it was not a huge succes,but it is now widely considered one of the most influential rock albums ever recorded. Rolling Stone has it listed as the 13th greatest album of all time.

    I. Chelsea Girls

    IV. The end

    After leaving The Velvet Underground Nico began work as a solo artist, performing regularly at The Dom in New York City. At these shows, she was accompanied by a revolving cast of guitarists.

    For her debut album, 1967’s Chelsea Girl, she recorded songs by Bob Dylan, Tim Hardin and Jackson Browne, among others. Velvet Underground members Lou Reed, John Cale and Sterling Morrison contributed to the album.

    Nico &

    Leonard Cohen

    During the 1960s, both Nico and Leanord Cohen were both fringe figures in Andy Warhol’s “Factory” crowd. Warhol speculated that Cohen had spent many time listening to Nico in clubs and that this had influenced his musical style a lot. Especially Chelsea Girl greatly influenced him.

    Nico also repeatedly spurned Cohen’s advances - preferring younger men - wich resulted in Cohen writing songs about her: “Memories”, “Joan Of Arc,” “Take This Longing,” and “One Of Us Cannot Be Wrong” are all inspired by Nico

    On the other hand, it was Cohen who introduced Nico to her signature instrument: the harmonium. His first impressions of her was that she looked like “the perfect Aryan ice queen”. He followed her all around New York.

    The Marble Index

    IV. The end

    For The Marble Index, her second solo album released in 1969, Nico wrote the lyrics and music. Accompaniment mainly centered around Nico’s harmonium, while John Cale added an array of folk and classical instruments, and arranged the album. The album has a classical-cum-European folk sound.

    Nico &

    Philippe Garrel

    Nico met Garrel in 1969 and contributed the song The Falconer to his film Le Lit de la Vierge.

    Soon after, she was living with Garrel and became a central figure in his cinematic and personal circles for about 10 years. During her relationship with Garrel Nico started using heroin, wich she was addicted to for over 15 years.

    Nico’s first acting appearance with Garrel occurred in his 1972 film, La Cicatrice Intérieure. Nico also supplied the music for this film, among others. and collaborated very closely with the director.

    Nico was his muse and source of inspiration. His 1991 film J’entends Plus la Guitare was completly dedicated to Nico.

    Desertshore & The end...

    IV. The end

    IV. The end

    In the 70’s Nico alternated more and more between her film career and her music career. She played in multiple movies and also released two more solo albums Desertshore (1970) and The End… (1974).

    She wrote the music, sang, and played the harmonium. John Cale produced and played most of the other instruments on both albums.

    These albums, along with The Marble Index, are now considered a trilogy.

    Nico &

    Siouxsie and the banshees

    Nico briefly toured as supporting act for the Proto-gothic rock band Siouxsie and the Banshees. Siouxsie and the Banshees were one of many post-punk bands who admired Nico.

    Nico’s album the marble index is sometimes described as “the first Goth album”. With its stark sound, somber lyrics, and Nico’s deliberate change in her look, the album became a crucial music and visual prototype for the gothic rock movement. Gothic rock also creates a dark atmosphere by drawing influence from the drones used by the Velvet Underground.

    Siouxsie Sioux’ foreboding vocals are influenced by Nico’s distinct dark style of singing.

    Drama of Exile & Camera obscure

    V. Drama of exile

    VI. Camera Obscura

    During the eighties Nico did periodic solo shows on the club circuit. In 1981 she released her first LP in seven years, Drama of Exile.

    She recorded what would be her last album, Camera Obscura, in 1985, again produced by John Cale.

    Leading a bohemian lifestyle, Nico floated from country to country, spending the last year of her life in Manchester, England. Reportedly, her methadone dependency had diminished and her songwriting had increased when she took her grown son, Ari, on holiday to the Spanish island of Ibiza.

    On the island Nico had a heart attack while riding a bicycle, and she hit her head as she fell; Misdiagnosed as having a sunstroke, she died there the next evening of a cerebral hemorrhage

    Punk legend Patti Smith has made the album “Killer road”, a narrative about this Tragic event as a tribute to Nico.

    After her death, maybe more than ever, artists look to Nico in search of inspiration. In this way Nico is still a muse, and will continue to be, as her icon grows ever larger.
